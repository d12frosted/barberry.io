Every rain is praised as a divine gift during these hot days in Kyiv. But as human beings we crave for solution we can predict and replicate on a whim. Luckily, we have wine and a technology to chill it. With this melting weather there is little wonder that 75% of wines I rated this month are white!

#+begin_quote
Wait, what? Do you count wines you drink and rate?
#+end_quote

Haha, of course I don't. But I do track every consumed wine in [[https://github.com/d12frosted/vino][a program]] that I've created for this purpose. And now that I have data and Application Programming Interface (API) provided by Vino, I can easily [[https://github.com/d12frosted/environment/tree/master/emacs#vino-stats][calculate various statistics]] related to my wine consumption. I plan to share these kind of reports on a monthly and yearly basis. Expect boring stats like amount of rated wines per country, grape, vintage, etc. But also expect full table of tasted wines along with the list of my favourites and outcasts of the month. Hope you find something interesting here. Share your ideas!

So this month has started with a beautiful [[barberry:/wines/ee17a380-0039-4cf6-acbb-c0d0a2875936][Girolamo Russo Etna Rosato 2019]], which I haven't tasted for almost a year. At this point of time I consider it one of my favourite light rosé wines despite confusingly mediocre impression when I tasted it for the first time - ☆ 7.25 vs ☆ 8.5!

The world of wine is big, and there is always something new to explore. So this month I have several personal novelties.

1. At [[barberry:/posts/2022-06-07-blind-tasting][a blind tasting event]], I tasted my first Pinot Noir from Alsace - [[barberry:/wines/f1137f23-9d0b-4e02-a8dc-aeef990ea592][J.M Dreyer Elios Pinot Noir 2020]].
2. [[barberry:/wines/268bd2f6-3e62-4a26-ba42-d514bc4e011d][Chandon Argentina Brut NV]] is my first traditional sparkling from Argentina.
3. After a long time of not drinking Bordeaux wines, and despite the hot weather, I actually tasted my first Margaux AOC - [[barberry:/wines/abe4809a-958e-46f3-8b58-5575332beb1e][Chateau d'Arsac Margaux Cru Bourgeois 2016]], and my first Saint-Estephe AOC - [[barberry:/wines/fd6559f6-41c5-4a3e-9de1-c88144358ccd][Chateau Laffitte Carcasset Saint-Estephe Cru Bourgeois 2016]]. After reading a little bit about these two left bank AOCs, I realised that my experience is pretty text-book, Margaux is refined and elegant, while Saint-Estephe is robust and rich.
4. I tasted Auxerrois blanc for the first time in my life - [[barberry:/wines/c7e19cc8-0f99-46b2-9f84-5375c933b593][Pierre Frick Crémant d'Alsace 2018]].
5. [[barberry:/wines/25826ae6-7e73-42f5-b2d3-5ce86b81b56b][Lucy Margaux Le Sauvignon Sensuel 2020]] is my first Sauvignon Blanc from Australia.

For the first half of the month, I rated every wine consumed, and I didn't taste any wine more than once during this period. But then I picked a few favourites for this month's table, and the ratio of rated to consumed normalised - 71%. So, according to my notes, I consumed *71* bottles and rated *53* of them. Only one of the wines was rated twice - [[barberry:/wines/558ec6f4-6d6c-4099-ad54-d55ad3099682][Golan Heights Winery Mount Hermon White 2019]], I just had too many bottles of this wine and wanted to share confirmation that it worth it.

Compared to previous month, it's a 82% of growth in terms of wines rated. Someone would think that it was a tough month for me, but I'd argue that I am just getting back to my normal numbers. Just take a look at the graph (first months of the war are excluded). Besides, this month I participated in a [[barberry:/posts/2022-06-07-blind-tasting][blind tasting event]] and [[barberry:/posts/2022-06-21-chenin-blanc-tasting][organised one myself]]. So this kind of explains the growth.

And now the most fascinating part - the favourites and outcasts of the month. Usually, this list matches the list of the best-rated/worst-rated wines, but sometimes I want to emphasise on a label or two. In June my favourites are:

- ★ 9.0 [[barberry:/wines/ca7dc126-0ea4-4245-93db-f07a87301a7e][Francoise Bedel L'Âme de la Terre Millésime 2006]] - truly amazing Champagne that displays effects of the long ageing on lees. The best sparkling wine of the month. Francoise Bedel is definitely one of my favourite producers, even her basic wines are delicious and worth buying.
- ★ 8.5 [[barberry:/wines/42dc355d-a934-4cb0-9592-cf1d474bec57][Sadie Family Pofadder 2020]] - the best red wine of the month, delicate, juicy and sophisticated. If only the price was lower!
- ★ 8.5 [[barberry:/wines/83d90838-5e63-43af-abc5-f5fb482bc36f][Domaine de la Taille Aux Loups Bretonniere Cuvée Parcellaire Monopole 2017]] - the best white wine of the month. Multilayered with amazingly long evolution of the taste. It still has potential, alas I don't have any bottles left.
- ★ 9.0 [[barberry:/wines/56317de6-f3c6-43f9-8efc-6537b23750c5][Viña Tondonia Blanco Reserva 2009]] - without comments, this is a legendary wine that is rightfully placed on this list.
- ★ 8.5 [[barberry:/wines/ee17a380-0039-4cf6-acbb-c0d0a2875936][Girolamo Russo Etna Rosato 2019]] - complex and delicious rosé for an attractive price, even after price adjustment.
- ★ 8.5 [[barberry:/wines/9513b9da-ac70-472c-953a-7cd9e5946b47][Sadie Family Skurfberg 2020]] - approachable and friendly yet complex and multilayered Chenin Blanc by skillful winemaker.
- ★ 7.5 [[barberry:/wines/558ec6f4-6d6c-4099-ad54-d55ad3099682][Golan Heights Winery Mount Hermon White 2019]] - my favourite table wine of the month. The price tag is not ideal, but the real value of UAH is yet to be discovered.

The outcasts of this month are those that disappointed me. I mean, there are wines that I rated lower, but that was totally fine and expected. But these labels...

- ★ 6.5 [[barberry:/wines/0aa4db7d-22bc-4e3e-876a-1740b7cfe73f][Costador Metamorphika Chenin Blanc 2017]] - might be a faulty bottle or serving issue, but still I have not expected it to has so many issues. I tried this wine on the second day, experience was slightly better, but still disappointing.
- ★ 7.0 [[barberry:/wines/268bd2f6-3e62-4a26-ba42-d514bc4e011d][Chandon Argentina Brut NV]] - despite being my first traditional sparkling from Argentina, experience was not fascinating. Mediocre crowd pleaser with high price tag. Not for me, not for you, believe me.
- ★ 7.0 [[barberry:/wines/224602d5-c307-4bfc-b84a-bfeede982fc0][COZs vn-c2 bg 2017]] - tasted blind and was shocked. I wanted to taste this wine for a long time, but now I am glad that I didn't buy a bottle myself. Overly simple and straight-forward. And I don't believe in it's potential.
- ★ 7.0 [[barberry:/wines/10032e11-4691-4634-8f36-ce7ed8c1dba2][Weingut Tement Temento Green 2020]] - one of my favourite table wines of the previous summer, this year is bland and soulless. I was almost crying.

And by the way, this month was not that bad if we consider the fact that the lowest rate is ★ 6.5!
